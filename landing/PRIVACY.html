Aside

Privacy Policy

Effective: 27 May 2026

Aside is a Chrome extension built on a single principle: your data stays on your device. This policy explains, in plain language, what that means in practice.

1. What Aside collects

Nothing. Aside has no analytics, no telemetry, no error reporting, no usage tracking, no first- or third-party cookies. The author has no servers and no way to see who installed the extension or what anyone does with it.

2. API keys

When you paste a provider API key into Settings, it is stored in Chrome's chrome.storage.local — the browser's encrypted per-extension storage on your device. Keys are only ever sent to the provider whose key it is (Anthropic, Google, OpenAI, xAI, Groq, or your local Ollama instance), over HTTPS, with the API request you initiated. Keys never reach any server operated by the author.

3. Page content

When you press an action that needs the page — Summarize, Key points, Translate, Extract, Find on page, or a custom prompt — Aside reads the visible text of the current tab, trims it to roughly 12,000 characters, and includes it in that single API request to your selected provider. Nothing is uploaded in the background. Nothing is sent unless you press a button.

4. Conversation history

Threads are saved per-origin in chrome.storage.local on your device. They are not synced to any cloud, are not visible to the author, and can be cleared from Settings or by uninstalling the extension.

5. Fully offline mode

Select Ollama as your provider and Aside makes no network calls outside localhost. Your prompts, your selected text, and the page content stay on your computer.

6. Third-party providers

When you choose a cloud provider, requests go directly from your browser to that provider's API. Their handling of your data is governed by their own privacy policy:

  • Anthropic (Claude)
  • Google (Gemini)
  • OpenAI (GPT-4o)
  • xAI (Grok)
  • Groq
  • Ollama runs locally and has no privacy policy because it sends nothing anywhere.

7. Permissions Aside requests

Aside is a Manifest V3 extension and requests only the permissions it needs to function: activeTab to read the current page when you ask, storage to save your settings and history locally, and host permissions for the six provider API endpoints listed in manifest.json. No broader access is requested.

8. Children

Aside is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any personal data from anyone.

9. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in any meaningful way, the new version will replace this page and the effective date above will be updated. Because Aside collects nothing, changes are likely to clarify wording rather than expand data handling.

10. Contact

Questions, security disclosures, or concerns: open an issue on GitHub.
